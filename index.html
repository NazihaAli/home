Chance the Rapper

Chance the Rapper is young American hip hop artist from Chicago. He raps, sings, songwrites, and plays the piano. Chance started his career in 2011 at the age of 17, and he has already seen much success with his music. Most recently, he won 3 awards for his third solo mixtape, “Coloring Book”, at the 59th Annual Grammy’s. Chance has collaborated with many other artists as well, such as:

  • Kanye West
  • Skrillex
  • Justin Bieber
  • Jeremih

Childhood

Chancelor Johnathan Bennett was born on April 16, 1993 in Chicago, Illinois (he is 23 years old). He lived in a middle class neighbourhood with his mother, father, and little brother Taylor, who is also a rapper. Growing up, he listened to artists like Kanye West, MC Hammer, Kirk Franklin, and many others. Chance went to Jones College Prep High School, where many of the teachers ridiculed his dreams of becoming a musician. However, he released his first mixtape, “10 Day”, shortly after and it was well received locally.

Career

2012-2013

Chance starting gaining recognition in 2012, when he was featured on one of Childish Gamibino’s records and joined his tour of North America. The next year, he released his second mixtape, titled “Acid Rap”, which was very highly acclaimed. The album received lots of praise from critics and from fans, and it has since been downloaded over 1 million times. Chance toured across the US late in 2013.

2014

Throughout 2014, Chance toured with many artists and helped promote brands. He also received the Chicago's “Outstanding Youth of the Year” award from the mayor, for his activism in his hometown. He joined many campaigns to help prevent gun violence in communities with his father. Chance even met with President Barack Obama at the White House to discuss the “My Brother’s Keepers Challenge”, an initiative that promotes racial justice.

2015

In 2015, Chance the Rapper released a short film called “Mr.Happy”, which is about a man dealing with depression. He also gave a lecture at Harvard University. Later that year, he released an album titled “Surf” in collaboration with many other artists, under the name of The Social Experiment. The album was met with lots of success, as well.

2016-Present

In 2016, Kanye West released his album “The Life of Pablo”, which Chance helped co-write. He also appeared on the track “Ultralight Beam”. On May 12, Chance released his third mixtape “Coloring Book”, which was only available for stream via Apple Music. In its first week, it was streamed over 57 million times and debuted at number 8 on the Billboard 200 chart. The mixtape was upbeat, with gospel and rap features. It included productions and collaborations with artists such as Lido, Kanye West, Young Thug, and Kirk Franklin. Coloring Book was very highly acclaimed and received an average score of 90 by music critics. It was also the first streaming-only album to ever win a Grammy. Chance was even promoted by H&M as the face of their new campaign. In late 2016, Chance the Rapper embarked on his “Magnificent Coloring” world tour.

Awards and Nominations

Chance the Rapper has been nominated for and has won many awards. Most famously, he was nominated for 7 Grammys and won 3 of them.

  • He has a little brother named Taylor who is also a rapper
  • He refuses to sign with a record label and earns most of his money touring
  • In the Cartoon Network series, “Black Dynamite”, Chance voiced Bob Marley
